Project Spillcry

Video

Executive Summary

Oil spills in the ocean damage the environment and marine life and unprecedented ways. The cost of cleaning these oil spills and mitigating their effects increases dramatically the longer the spills go undetected. This cost comes in many forms, including damage to the environment, hurt reputations of oil companies, and lost revenue from various businesses whose products and services rely on oil or coastal regions (such as fishers, marine merchants, and tourism companies). Project Spillcry aims to develop a monitoring system capable of detecting and assessing oil spills occurring near US coasts and alerting relevant third parties (such as governments, oil companies, and environmental organizations). Through a long process of functional analysis, concept generation, and further development, Project Spillcry evolved into a system of eight UAVs that will inspect spill-vulnerable areas, utilizing computer vision and Gaussian linear approximations to detect and assess spills while communicating with third parties via the internet.
